U.S. and China Headed for Fight Over Iran Oil? Not So Fast.

中美两国会因为伊朗石油问题闹翻吗？

2012年06月20日16:52

（本文版权归道琼斯公司所有，未经许可不得翻译或转载。）

The U.S. decision last week to leave China off a list of seven additional countries exempt from looming U.S. sanctions on Iran has led to questions about how the U.S. and China will resolve issue before the end-of-June deadline. The Obama administration has said talks are still ongoing with China.

美国上周宣布又有七个国家免于因伊朗石油问题受到美国制裁，但中国并不在这个名单当中，这让外界产生疑问：美国和中国将如何在6月底最后期限到来之前解决问题。奥巴马政府说，与中国的谈判仍在进行之中。

Under the new sanctions, foreign financial institutions doing business with Iran's central bank for the purpose of energy purchases stand to be barred from U.S. financial markets. Countries such as India, Japan and South Korea were all granted exemptions after showing they significantly reduced imports.

按照新的制裁措施，为购买能源之目的而与伊朗央行有业务往来的外国金融机构将被禁止进入美国金融市场。在表明已经大幅减少了从伊朗进口石油的规模之后，印度日本和韩国等国家获得了豁免权。

China, however, is Iran's largest buyer of crude, receiving 22% of Iran's exports in the first half of 2011. China Petrochemical Corp., known as Sinopec Group, and Zhuhai Zhenrong Co., are the two main importers, and attention has focused on Sinopec, which imported about 280,000 barrels a day of crude oil and condensate into China in 2011. China has repeatedly said its oil imports from Iran aren't in violation of U.N. Security Council resolutions and that it doesn't recognize unilateral sanctions.

Reuters然而，中国是伊朗石油的最大买家，2011年上半年，伊朗出口的石油有22%流入了中国。中国石油化工集团公司(China Petrochemical Corp., 简称：中石化集团)和珠海振戎公司(Zhuhai Zhenrong Co.)是从伊朗进口石油的主要企业。2011年中石化每天进口大约28万桶原油和凝析油，因此人们的目光都聚焦在了中石化身上。中国反复强调，中国从伊朗进口石油没有违反联合国安理会的决议，并且中国不接受将单边制裁强加于第三国的做法。

China Real Time report spoke with Erica Downs, an expert on China's state-owned energy companies at the Brookings Institution, to get her take. Edited excerpts:

《华尔街日报》的“中国实时报”(China Real Time)栏目就这一问题采访了布鲁金斯学会(Brookings Institution)研究中国国有石油公司的专家邓丽嘉(Erica Downs)，以下是经过编辑的访谈节录：

China Real Time: Will the U.S. require China to cut Iranian imports further or will it simply accept a promise that China won't raise its imports?

中国实时报：美国是否会要求中国进一步削减从伊朗进口原油，还是中国只要承诺不增加伊朗石油的进口就可以了？

Erica Downs: I suspect one of the reasons that China wasn't among the countries granted exemptions to U.S. sanctions on the Central Bank of Iran last week is because of uncertainty in Washington about China's intentions with respect to crude imports from Iran. China's purchases of Iranian crude dropped more than 30% during the first four months of 2012.

邓丽嘉：我认为，中国上周没有获得豁免权的原因之一是美国不确定中国从伊朗进口原油的打算。2012年头四个月，中国从伊朗购买原油的数量下降了30%还多。

Why wasn't this enough to secure China a waiver? Probably because the reason for the decline was a contract dispute between Sinopec and National Iranian Oil Co., rather than an explicit decision to support American efforts to pressure Iran. Sinopec recently revealed that is targeting a 16%-20% reduction in its purchases of Iranian crude this year, a level on par with the 15%-22% percent cut pledged by Japan to secure an exemption earlier this year. However, Sinopec will likely meet its target primarily as a result of the drop in imports during the contract dispute with NIOC.

那么为什么这还不足以让中国获得豁免权呢？也许是因为，进口量下降的原因是中石化和伊朗国有石油公司(National Iranian Oil Co.)出现了合同纠纷，并不代表中国明确支持美国对伊朗施压的努力。中石化最近宣布，计划今年把从伊朗进口的原油数量减少16%到20%，这个幅度与日本今年早些时候为获得豁免权承诺的15%到22%的水平不相上下。不过，中石化实现其目标很可能是因为与伊朗国有石油公司存在合同纠纷。

The fact that China's crude imports from Iran are now on the rise (Sinopec finally signed a new contract with NIOC in late March) may make it a bit more difficult for the Obama administration to grant China an exemption, especially because it is an election year. That said, I suspect China will get one by the June 28 deadline.

中国从伊朗进口原油的数量正在增加（中石化最终在3月末与伊朗国有石油公司签订了一份新合同），这一事实可能会让奥巴马政府更加难以做出给中国以豁免权的决定，特别是在今年这个大选年。尽管如此，我猜中国会在6月28日最后期限之前获得豁免权。

CRT: What Chinese financial institutions may be exposed to the new U.S. sanctions?

中国实时报：中国的哪些金融机构可能会受到新的美国制裁的影响？

ED: China's major commercial banks are very cautious about Iran. They don't want to jeopardize the opportunity to expand in the U.S. market. This is especially true for Bank of China Ltd. If there are any Chinese banks currently doing business with the Central Bank of Iran, they are probably small banks with little or no exposure to the U.S., such as Bank of Kunlun Co.

邓丽嘉：中国主要商业银行在与伊朗合作问题上十分谨慎。他们不想毁掉在美国市场扩张的机会，中国银行(Bank of China)尤其如此。如果现在还有中资银行在与伊朗央行做生意，这些银行只可能是一些小银行，而且与美国很少有或几乎没有业务往来，比如昆仑银行(Bank of Kunlun Co.)。

CRT: How much influence does Sinopec have over Chinese government policy toward Iran and the U.S.?

中国实时报：在中国政府制定针对伊朗和美国的政策方面，中石化有多大影响力？

ED: Sinopec is a powerful company that, at times, undoubtedly lobbies the Chinese government to take actions to advance Sinopec's interests. But China's national oil companies ultimately follow the guidance issued by China's leaders, especially on sensitive foreign policy issues. For example, in 2010, the Chinese government reportedly told China's national oil companies (NOCs) to slow down their upstream activities in Iran. Even though China's NOCs may have been tempted to take over some of the projects abandoned by European and Japanese oil companies, they didn't do so. Moreover, they have made so little progress on their own projects in Iran that the Iranians have threatened to void at least one of the contracts unless work is sped up.

邓丽嘉：中石化是个有实力的公司，有时的确会游说中国政府采取行动，借此为公司谋取利益。但中国的国有石油公司最终都会遵循中国领导人给出的指导意见，特别是在敏感的外交政策问题上。例如2010年，据报道中国政府曾一再让国有石油公司放慢在伊朗的上游活动的步伐。虽然国有石油公司可能有兴趣接手欧洲和日本石油企业所放弃的部分伊朗项目，但它们并没有这么做。而且，它们自己的伊朗项目进展也十分缓慢，伊朗方面曾威胁称，如果不加快项目步伐，就会将至少一份合同宣布作废。

CRT: Can Sinopec commercially afford to reduce crude imports from Iran?

中国实时报：从商业角度看，如果削减从伊朗进口原油的规模，中石化能否承受？

ED: Washington's efforts to get buyers of Iranian crude to reduce their purchases has put Sinopec in a tough position. Last year, China imported more than 550,000 barrels per day from Iran. Most -- if not all -- of this oil ended up in Sinopec's refineries. It would be difficult for Sinopec to replace such a large volume of crude.

邓丽嘉：华盛顿努力让伊朗原油的买家削减采购规模，中石化因此陷入艰难处境。去年，中国每天从伊朗进口原油超过55万桶，其中绝大多数（不是全部）最终进了中石化的炼油厂。让中石化找到可替代的如此大规模的原油供应不是件容易的事。

CRT: Will Sinopec's crude trade with Iran jeopardize its current or future energy investments in the U.S.?

中国实时报：中石化与伊朗的原油贸易是否会对该公司眼下或未来在美国的能源投资产生负面影响？

In my view, one of the major geopolitical benefits to Washington of the recent upstream investments made by Sinopec and Cnooc Ltd. in the U.S. is that these investments will likely make both companies even more cautious with respect to Iran. Fu Chengyu, the chairman of Sinopec, is acutely aware of how getting on the wrong side of Congress can scuttle a deal; he was the head of Cnooc when Cnooc made its ill-fated bid for Unocal Corp in 2005. Fu undoubtedly knows that if Sinopec steps up its upstream activities in Iran or dramatically increases its oil purchases from Iran, such moves would jeopardize Sinopec's chances to expand in the U.S. And the U.S., with its booming shale gas industry, is an attractive destination for Sinopec, which plans to expand the role of natural gas, including shale gas, in its portfolio.

邓丽嘉：在我看来，中石化与中海油(Cnooc Ltd.)近来在美国的上游投资带给华盛顿的主要地缘政治好处之一，是这些投资可能会让这两家公司在处理伊朗问题时更加谨慎。中石化董事长傅成玉很清楚，在美国国会站错队是会把交易搅黄的。2005年，中海油收购美国加州联合石油公司(Unocal Corp)遇挫，当时执掌中海油的正是傅成玉。他肯定知道，如果中石化加快在伊朗上游项目的步伐，或大幅增加从伊朗采购石油的规模，该公司在美国的扩张机会就会受到不利影响。美国页岩气产业现在正迅速发展，因此美国是一个对中石化有吸引力的目的地。中石化目前打算扩大天然气（包括页岩气）在公司的投资组合中所起的作用。

CRT: What incentive does China have to cooperate with the U.S. over Iran?

中国实时报：有什么会促使中国就伊朗问题与美国展开合作吗？

China's relationship with the U.S. is China's most important bilateral relationship. While Beijing finds Washington's unilateral sanctions to be distasteful and is probably tired of repeatedly being asked by Washington to support new U.S. unilateral sanctions on Iran, Beijing also doesn't want Iran to become a major source of tension in the U.S.-China relationship.

邓丽嘉：中美关系是中国最重要的双边关系。虽然华盛顿实施单边制裁令北京不悦，而且北京很可能也厌倦了华盛顿一再要求它支持美国对伊朗新的单边制裁措施，但北京并不希望伊朗成为美中关系上一个主要的矛盾来源。
